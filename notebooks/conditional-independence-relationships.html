Conditional Independence Relationships

  • Historical Origins

    • A. P. Dawid
      • A. P. Dawid, "Conditional Independence in Statistical Theory," Journal of the Royal Statistical Society Series B, vol. 41, no. 1, pp. 1–31.
        • Also the origin (?) of the \(\perp\,\!\,\!\,\!\,\!\,\!\,\!\,\!\!\!\!\perp\) notation for \( X \perp\,\!\,\!\,\!\,\!\,\!\,\!\,\!\!\!\!\perp Y\) to indicate that \(X\) and \(Y\) are independent.
    • Wolfgang Spohn
      • 1980 paper
    • Judea Pearl

  • Relationship to Information Theory

    • Can largely be 'read off' from the definitions of conditional independence presented in Dawid, and knowledge of how to manipulate entropies.

  • Relationship to Computational Mechanics

    • Shalizi and Crutchfield C. R. Shalizi and J. P. Crutchfield, "Computational Mechanics: Pattern and Prediction, Structure and Simplicity,” Journal of Statistical Physics, 2001.
