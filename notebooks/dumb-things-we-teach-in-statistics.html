Examples: * In large samples, performing a normality test before using a two sample T-test for difference in population means. If we reject normality, then we use a nonparametric test for difference in centers instead. But with a large enough sample, we'll likely reject normality for even small deviations from normality. ("A goodness-of-fit test under a fixed distribution is ultimately a measure of sample size.") Yet, because of the sample size, the Central Limit Theorem has kicked in for the sample means, so the T-test will work regardless of the underlying distribution of the data (unless the data are pathological). Takeaway: with a large enough sample, a T-test will suffice for testing differences of population means. * See: R.
