Possible Pitfalls in the Estimation of Transfer Entropy

  • Interpretational:
    • D. A. Smirnov, "Spurious causalities with transfer entropy," Phys. Rev. E, vol. 87, no. 4, p. 042917, Apr. 2013.
      • A nice illustration of how 'apparent' transfer entropies, using the terminology of Lizier, can lead to spurious conclusions in the presence of (a) hidden state variables, (b) observational noise, or (c) insufficient temporal resolution. This is done with exact transfer entropies, so we know that the `spurious' values are not artifacts of finite data. The systems considered are as simple as possible, to allow for this analytical treatment: Gaussian autoregressive processes and discrete-state Markov processes. Brilliant paper, the sort I would like to write.
    • R. G. James, N. Barnett, and J. P. Crutchfield, "Information Flows? A Critique of Transfer Entropies." arXiv
