Volume Conduction in EEG/MEG Studies

Description of the problem.

A related problem: the use of a common reference for computing voltage differences can introduce spuriously detected synchronization.

  • References from the Nonlinear Dynamics World:

    • S. Bialonski and M. T. Horstmann, "From brain to earth and climate systems: small-world interaction networks or not?," Chaos, 2010.
      • Their 'common source problem' is a neuroscientist's 'volume conduction.'

  • References from Neuroscience:

    • R. Vicente, M. Wibral, M. Lindner, and G. Pipa, “Transfer entropy-a model-free measure of effective connectivity for the neurosciences,” Journal of computational neuroscience, 2011.
      • One of their computational experiments considers the effect of volume conduction on transfer entropy. See Section 2.3.3.
