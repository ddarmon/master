Comparing two population means

  1. Give examples of scientific questions that would warrant comparisons of two population means.
  2. Recognize the Greek letter \(\delta\), the Greek analog to the Roman letter \(d\), which is used to indicate \(\delta\text{ifferences}\) between population parameters.
  3. State a claim about two population means \(\mu_{X}\) and \(\mu_{Y}\) as an equality / inequality involving the difference \(\delta = \mu_{X} - \mu_{Y}\) between the population means.

Two-sample \(t\) procedures

  1. State the test statistic used in the two-sample \(t\)-test.
  2. Determine, qualitatively, whether an observed test statistic from the two-sample \(t\)-test provides evidence against a null hypothesis.
  3. State the sampling distribution of the test statistic used in the two-sample \(t\)-test, and the assumptions that must hold for that sampling distribution to be correct.
