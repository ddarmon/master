Instructions for Downloading and Uploading Anki Decks to eCampus

The following bullets lay out the submission instructions and grading procedure I will use for the Anki decks.

They are a bit draconian, and the tl;dr is this:

tl;dr: Make sure you are regularly using Anki (at least once per day) and submit your Anki decks before each Wednesday class, and you will receive the full 5% of the course points allotted to pre-class preparation.

Download and Submission Instructions

  • New Anki decks will be provided on eCampus at the end of each class session.
    • You should load these into your local version of Anki by the end of that day.
  • Your Anki decks are to be uploaded to eCampus by 11:30 AM each Wednesday.
    • In Anki, use File > Export... > Export format: Anki Deck Package (*.apkg) to export all of your Anki decks[1].
    • Make sure you have Course Decks::MA 220 selected in Include: and the Include scheduling information check box is selected.
    • Upload the *.apkg file to the appropriate Anki Deck - Due by Beginning of Lecture _ assignment under Assessments > Assignments.
  • After each Wednesday class, I will randomly select 6 students, and review their Anki decks.
    • If you are selected, I will review your deck history to make sure you are using Anki regularly and appropriately (e.g. not just cruising through "Show Answer > Good" at a breakneck pace for each question[2]), which will determine your grade for that submission.
      • If I find you have not been using Anki regularly and appropriately, I may go back and review previous submissions for which you were not one of the selected students.
    • If you are not selected, your grade will be determined by whether you submitted your Anki decks.
  • You have a 40% chance of being selected each week, and will be selected, on average, 4.8 times over the course of the semester.
    • I provide these statistics for full transparency, not to encourage you to try to game the system.

[1] If you are already using Anki, good job! Unless you want to share your personal decks with me (I wouldn't recommend it), you can create a separate profile for use with this class by selecting File > Switch Profile, and selecting Add from the dialog box.

[2] If you can remember everything instantly on the first try, congratulations! But I will expect this at-home performance to be reflected on your homework, quizzes, and exams.
